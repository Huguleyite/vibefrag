Hawas Highness

by Rasasi

Amber and citrus up front, sharp enough to notice, warm enough to hold.

Amber and citrus open Hawas Highness sharp enough to notice, warm enough to hold. The spice up top does the heavy lifting, keeping it from reading as a straight fruit-and-amber cologne.

It moves into jasmine, rose, and violet at the heart — more floral than most colognes in this price range bother with — then settles into cedarwood, patchouli, and vetiver. Dry, grounded, built to last.

One early reviewer described it as landing between two familiar references, and that tracks: Hawas Highness borrows its amber-citrus spine from Sospiro Vibrato while carrying its own weight in the base.

$45 at Jomashop for 100ml against what a niche house like Sospiro usually charges — that's the whole pitch.
